#   1. Most visually distinctive feature FIRST (CLIP weights early tokens heavily)
#   2. Then: color → texture → morphology → body location → unique differentiators
#   3. One condition per line. Lines starting with # are comments (ignored by server).
#   4. Be specific. "honey-colored crust" beats "skin problem".
#   5. Include what makes it DIFFERENT from similar conditions.

#  SECTION 1: FUNGAL INFECTIONS
# ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

# --- Tinea (Dermatophytoses) ---
# CLIP anchor: ring shape + scaly border + central clearing
tinea corporis — circular ring-shaped scaly red patch with a raised border and clear center, also known as body ringworm
tinea capitis — scaly patch of hair loss on the scalp with broken hairs and gray scales, common in children
tinea pedis — peeling, cracked, and scaly skin between the toes, also known as athlete's foot
tinea cruris — half-moon shaped red-brown scaly patch on the inner thigh, also known as jock itch
tinea unguium — thick, yellow-brown, and brittle nail with crumbling debris underneath
tinea versicolor — small, discolored patches (lighter or darker) with fine scales, usually on the chest and back

# --- Other fungi ---
# CLIP anchor: unusual shape/color not matched by common conditions
candidiasis — bright red, moist skin with small white bumps, typically in skin folds
oral thrush candidiasis — white, creamy patches on the tongue and inside of the mouth
deep fungal mycetoma — chronic foot swelling with draining sores that release colored particles
chromoblastomycosis — slow-growing, warty, cauliflower-like bump, usually on the lower leg
sporotrichosis — a chain of bumps that appear in a line along a lymphatic channel, often after a rose thorn prick

#  SECTION 2: PARASITIC INFESTATIONS
# ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

# CLIP anchor: tracks / burrows / geometric pattern
scabies — very itchy, S-shaped tracks between the fingers, on the wrist, or on the genitals
cutaneous larva migrans — winding, snake-like red track on the foot that moves a few millimeters per day
pediculosis capitis — small white eggs (nits) attached to hair shafts near the scalp, causing itching
tungiasis jigger flea — black dot inside a swollen white ring, usually on the tip of a toe or the sole of the foot
onchocerciasis — firm bumps under the skin with rough, thick, and itchy dark skin, sometimes with patches of lighter skin
loiasis — temporary swelling and a visible worm moving across the white of the eye
lymphatic filariasis — severe swelling and thickened, warty skin on a lower limb, creating a "mossy" texture
myiasis — swollen, boil-like bump with a central opening, where a botfly larva is present
cutaneous leishmaniasis — painless sore with a raised, crater-like border that leaves a scar

tropical ulcer — deep, painful ulcer with a sloughing base and ragged edges, usually on the lower leg
Buruli ulcer — large, painless, open ulcer with wide, undermined edges
leprosy tuberculoid — single, light-colored patch with a raised edge and no feeling, indicating nerve damage
leprosy lepromatous — multiple, widespread nodules on the face, earlobes, and limbs, causing a "lion-like" appearance

# CLIP anchor: comedone type + location + absence/presence of inflammation
# NOTE: Acne vs Rosacea is the hardest pair — key differentiator = comedones (acne YES, rosacea NO)
acne vulgaris — a mix of blackheads, whiteheads, and pimples on oily skin
acne comedonal — multiple blackheads and whiteheads with very little inflammation

rhinophyma — enlarged, red, and bulbous nose with thick, pitted skin
perioral dermatitis — small, red bumps around the mouth, with a clear area right next to the lips
hidradenitis suppurativa — painful, deep bumps and abscesses in the armpits and groin, which can lead to scarring and tunnels under the skin
pseudofolliculitis barbae — ingrown hairs in the beard area that cause bumps and pimples, common in Black skin
